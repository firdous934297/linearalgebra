                Early Developments:


                Ancient Civilizations:
                The study of linear equations, particularly systems of them, emerged early in the history of mathematics.

                Babylonians (around 1800 BC) and the Chinese (around 200 BC) developed methods for solving such systems. 


                Determinants:
                Gottfried Leibniz is credited with introducing the notion of a determinant in 1693, a concept crucial for solving

                systems of linear equations. 

                Cramer's Rule:
                Gabriel Cramer, in 1750, used determinants to solve systems of equations, leading to the development of Cramer's Rule. 
                Matrices and Vectors:

                The term "matrix" was introduced by James Joseph Sylvester in 1848, while the concept of vectors, a fundamental part

                of linear algebra, was developed by Hermann Grassmann in his "Theory of Extension" published in 1844.

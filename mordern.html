                 Development of Modern Linear Algebra:
                 
                 Quaternions:
                 William Rowan Hamilton's discovery of quaternions in 1843, which added imaginary units to real numbers,
 
                 laid groundwork for the development of linear algebra concepts.


                 Vector Spaces: 
                 The concept of vector spaces, a core concept in modern linear algebra, emerged in the late 19th century.
 

                 Influence of Physics and Engineering:

                 The practical applications of linear algebra in physics and engineering,

                 particularly during World War II, led to its increased importance and wider adoption.
